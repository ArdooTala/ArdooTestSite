CURRENT WASTE MANAGEMENT SOLUTIONS

Solution: LANDFILL
Is it even considered a solution?

Material life-cycle: Landfill as the solution to the waste management

  • Water and land pollution.
  • Transportation costs and carbon-footprint.
  • Negative environmental impacts.

A landfill site is a site for the disposal of waste materials by burial. Landfills provide “out-of-sight-out-of-mind” rationality to the problem of waste management. After it leaves your home, most people ignore what happens to it. Once it is buried in a landfill, we are inclined to forget about it. This is a problem because all landfills have inherent problems related to contaminants escaping and affecting our environment.
If recyclable materials are put into landfill sites you use more energy and resources trying to create new materials instead of re-using or recycling so landfill sites should not be the first port of call for re-usable or recyclable materials.

Solution: BURNING
Escalating the problem

Material life-cycle: Burning as the solution to the waste management

  • Air pollution.
  • Transportation costs and carbon-footprint.
  • High energy consumption.

Over the years the process of waste management and recycling has gained increased attention around the world and in the country specifically. Accelerated growth and increase in population, as well as the increase in income per person, increase in industrial development and economic activities, have led to the emergence of new consumption and production habits and negative environmental behaviorism. One of the most harmful behaviors is waste burning.
Burning trash in an uncontrolled manner would release all sorts of pollutants into the environment, which would have significant negative impacts on air quality, water quality, health and so on. As well, not everything that is in the waste stream is easily burned.

Solution: RECYCLING
The best possible solution yet

Material life-cycle: Recycling as the solution to the waste management

  • Requires special industrial facilities.
  • Transportation costs and carbon-footprint.
  • High energy consumption.

Recycling is the process of converting waste materials into new materials and objects. It is an alternative to "conventional" waste disposal that can save material and help lower greenhouse gas emissions. Recycling can prevent the waste of potentially useful materials and reduce the consumption of fresh raw materials, thereby reducing: energy usage, air pollution (from incineration), and water pollution (from landfilling). recycling aims at environmental sustainability by substituting raw material inputs into and redirecting waste outputs out of the economic system.

What is the problem with recycling?

Recycling is either a chemical process, mechanical process, thermal process, or a mix of the afore-mentioned. All these processes require special industrial facilities which in turn requires a whole factory. This in turn means transportation of the waste material. In addition, all the above-mentioned protocols are usually highly energy-consumpting which results in higher cost and makes it un-beneficial for most of the materials.
